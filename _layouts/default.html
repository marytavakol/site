Maryam Tavakol

I am an Assistant Professor at Uncertainty in AI group in the department of Math & Computer Science of Eindhoven University of Thechnology. Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher in the AI group at TU Dortmund. Before that, I was a member of Machine Learning group at Leuphana University of Lüneburg as a Research Assistant, and at the same time, pursuing my PhD in Machine Learning in the Computer Science department of TU Darmstadt. During my PhD, I spent a six-month internship at the Recommendation team of Criteo Company in France.

I received both of my B.Sc. and M.Sc degrees in Computer Science from the University of Tehran, Iran.

last update: October 2020


I am fascinated with Machine Learning, both in theory and application. My research focus is mainly on Reinforcement Learning and Multi-armed Bandits that I employ in various domains ranging from Recommender Systems to Sport Analysis and Natural Language Processing. Recently, I am more inclined toward Counterfactual Learning (off-policy) in RL and bandits that are more applicable in real-world scenarios. Additionally, I am interesetd in exploring the areas of responsible AI, and more specificly, Fairness-aware Learning.
